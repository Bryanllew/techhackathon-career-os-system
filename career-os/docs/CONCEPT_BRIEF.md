# Career OS — Concept Brief

## The Problem

Job boards see your last role. Recruiters see your last title. Both reduce your career to a single point in time — your past. They miss what actually matters: your trajectory.

This creates three problems. First, candidates chase roles they qualify for on paper but that lead nowhere. Second, employers screen for keyword matches and miss people whose arc points directly at the role they need to fill. Third, there is no shared language between the two sides. Candidates think in terms of "what I want next." Employers think in terms of "what we need now." These two conversations don't meet.

The result is a labor market that matches résumés to job descriptions. Not people to opportunities.

## What Career OS Does

Career OS replaces the résumé with a trajectory graph. It maps where a person is going — not just where they have been — and matches them to roles that sit on that path.

For candidates, Career OS is a career operating system. It shows the real paths that people with similar profiles have taken, the skills those transitions required, and the roles currently open that align with the trajectory. It surfaces the concrete next steps — a certification, a portfolio project, a stakeholder presentation — that turn a goal into a sequence of actions.

For employers, Career OS is a workforce intelligence layer. It tracks team engagement signals, identifies internal flight risks, and surfaces candidates whose trajectories align with open roles — including past applicants whose skills have since caught up. It answers a question no ATS answers: who is ready for this role now who was not ready six months ago?

## How It Works

Three systems sit beneath the surface.

**Trajectory Mapping.** Career OS models career paths as sequences of milestones, each defined by a role, an unlocking skill, and a typical salary band. The paths are drawn from real transition data — 50,000+ trajectories from people who made similar moves. A data analyst considering three futures (Data Scientist, Product Analyst, BI Manager) sees not just job titles but timelines, median salaries, and the skills that made each path work for the people who took it.

**Skills Gap Analysis.** For each target role, Career OS compares the market's current requirements — extracted from live job postings — against the candidate's existing skills. It assigns a fit score, categorizes gaps into "in progress" and "missing," and recommends learning resources ranked by demand. The analysis updates as the market shifts, so the gaps reflect what employers are asking for right now, not last year.

**Kanban Pipeline + Deal Room.** The job search becomes a six-stage pipeline (Saved → Networking → Applying → Applied → Interviewing → Offer). Each card carries contextual signals: fit tier, days in stage, next action required. Clicking a card opens a Personal Deal Room — a side panel with seven tabs that consolidate everything about that opportunity: role overview, interview tips, AI tools, key contacts, similar jobs, private notes, and company context. The job search stops being a list and becomes a workflow.

On the employer side, the same trajectory data powers re-engagement. Career OS monitors past candidates and flags when their trajectory has shifted closer to an open role — turning a "no for now" into a "ready now."

## What Makes It Different

Most career tools answer "what jobs exist?" Career OS answers "what path should you take?" The difference is the time horizon. A job board optimizes for the next click. Career OS optimizes for the next five years.

The dual-sided design matters too. When a candidate builds skills toward a Data Scientist role, they become more visible to employers hiring for that role — not because they applied, but because their trajectory fits. When an employer identifies that a team member is disengaging, Career OS can flag internal roles or development paths before the person leaves. Both sides benefit from the same trajectory data, viewed through different lenses.

## Where It Is Now

Career OS is a working prototype. The candidate experience is fully built: kanban pipeline, personal deal rooms, trajectory mapping with interactive career maps, skills gap analysis with market demand data, and smart apply tools. The employer experience includes a dashboard with team signals, candidate search, active role management, re-engagement tracking, and workforce planning. The design language is dark iOS glass morphism — translucent surfaces, backdrop blur, and a design token system that can scale across the full product.

The prototype is live at [career-os-olive.vercel.app](https://career-os-olive.vercel.app).
